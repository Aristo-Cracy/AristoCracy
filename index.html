Introduction

Welcome to our clan! We are a group of dedicated Clash of Clans players who share a passion for strategy, teamwork, and having fun. Our clan is committed to creating a positive and supportive environment where members can grow as players and forge lasting friendships. Whether you're a seasoned player or just starting out, we welcome you to join us and become part of our community. In this webpage, we have outlined the guidelines, rules, and other important information that will help you understand how our clan operates and what is expected of its members. Let's work together to build a strong and successful clan!

#This webpage contains information about the responsibilities, duties, and expectations for and from every individual of the clan. It is important for each and every one to familiarize themselves with this information and to adhere to these guidelines in order to fulfill their roles effectively.



1. Requirements

To become a member of our clan, we have the following requirements that must be met:

  1. Townhall level: Currently, our minimum town hall requirement to join our clan is Town Hall 9 (TH9) or higher. This requirement is in place to ensure that our members have access to the troops, spells, and defenses needed to participate actively in clan wars, clan games, and other events.
  2. Minimum trophy count: We require a minimum trophy count of 2200 to join our clan. This ensures that our members are active and have experience playing the game.
  3. Donation requirements: We expect our members to donate troops to other members on a regular basis. Members must donate at least 1200(40 troop capacity per day) troops per season.
  4. Respectful behavior: We expect all members to treat each other with respect and refrain from any negative behavior or language. Discriminatory or derogatory comments will not be tolerated.
  5. Activity: We expect members to be active in the game and in the clan. Everyone is expected to have atleat 90 attacks in every season. Members should participate in clan wars, clan games, and donate troops regularly. Inactive members may be subject to removal from the clan.
  6. Age requirement: We require all members to be at least 16 years old to join our clan.
By meeting these requirements, you will become a valued member of our clan and contribute to our success.


2. Language

Our clan welcomes members from all over the world, and as such, we have a diverse community of players who may speak different languages. While English is the primary language used in the clan chat, we understand that not all members may be fluent in English.

We encourage all members to communicate in English as much as possible, to ensure that everyone can understand and participate in the conversation. However, we understand that language barriers can be a challenge, and we will make every effort to accommodate members who may have difficulty communicating in English.

If a member is not fluent in English, we encourage them to communicate in their native language as much as possible, as long as they are still able to follow our rules and guidelines. In such cases, we may rely on translation tools to help us communicate effectively.

We believe that diversity and inclusivity are important values for our clan, and we are committed to creating a welcoming and supportive environment for all members, regardless of their language or background. By working together and communicating effectively, we can build a strong and active community that supports each other and achieves our common goals.


3. Donations & Receives

3.1. Donations

Donations are an essential part of our clan's success, and we expect all members to participate in the donation system. Donating troops is a great way to help your fellow clan members and earn XP, and it also helps us win wars and clan games.

When making donations, we have a few guidelines that we ask members to follow:

  1. Donate only what is requested: When a member requests troops, they will specify which troops they need. Please donate only the troops that are requested, as this ensures that members receive the troops they need for their attacks.
  2. Donate high-level troops: Whenever possible, please donate high-level troops that are useful in war attacks. This includes troops such as eds, super dragons, dragons, hounds, witches, wizards, super minions, etc.
  3. Don't be a hog: While it's great to receive donations, please don't be greedy when requesting troops. Be considerate of other members and only request what you need for your attacks.

3.2. Receives

In addition to making donations, all members will also receive troops from other members. When receiving troops, we ask that members do the following:

  1. Thank the donor: When you receive troops, take a moment to thank the member who donated them. This helps build camaraderie within the clan and encourages members to continue donating.
  2. Use the troops wisely: Use the troops you receive in war attacks or for farming, and don't waste them on unnecessary battles.
By following these guidelines for donations and receives, we can ensure that our clan remains strong and successful.


4. Clan Wars

Clan wars are an important part of our clan's activities, and we take them seriously. They are a great way to test our skills and teamwork, and to earn loot and rewards for our clan.

To participate in clan wars, members must opt-in by selecting the "I'm In" button in the clan war screen. Once the war has started, members will be matched up against an opposing clan and will have a set amount of time to make their attacks.

Here are some guidelines for participating in clan wars:

  1. Use both attacks: All members who opt-in to a war are expected to use both of their attacks. This ensures that we have the best chance of winning the war.
  2. Attack appropriate targets: Members should only attack bases that are appropriate for their level and skill. We will assign targets based on your level and strength.
  3. Communicate with your clanmates: Communication is key during clan wars. If you're unsure about which base to attack, ask for advice from your clanmates. If you're unable to make your attacks, let us know as soon as possible so we can adjust our strategy.
  4. Be patient: Sometimes wars don't go as planned, and we may lose battles. Don't get discouraged and keep a positive attitude. We'll learn from our mistakes and come back stronger in the next war.
We take clan wars seriously and expect all members to do their best to help the clan win. By working together and communicating effectively, we can achieve great things in clan wars.


5. Clan Capital

Clan Capital is a special structure in Clash of Clans that can be built by clan members using resources obtained through raids. It provides a bonus to clan members during clan wars and can also be upgraded to increase its effectiveness. To ensure success during a raid weekend, clan members should prioritize attacking enemy bases, obtaining as many resources as possible, and contributing them to the clan to build and upgrade the Clan Capital.

Things a member should do during a raid weekend:

  1. Members should be prepared and strategize their attacks by scouting enemy bases and discussing strategies with other members.
  2. Members should prioritize attacking enemy bases that are within their skill level to maximize successful attacks.
  3. Contributing resources obtained from raid attacks can help build and upgrade the Clan Capital.
  4. Members should actively communicate and coordinate with each other during the raid to maximize the collective success of the clan.


6. Clan Games

Clan Games is a special event in Clash of Clans that allows clan members to work together to complete challenges and earn rewards. The event lasts for a set period of time and offers various tasks that require players to either attack other bases, collect resources or donate troops.

To participate in Clan Games, members must earn a minimum of 900 points. These points are earned by completing challenges, and each challenge has a set number of points associated with it.

Here are some guidelines for participating in Clan Games:

  1. Work together: Clan Games require teamwork to be successful. Members should communicate with each other and coordinate their efforts to maximize their points.
  2. Focus on high point challenges: Some challenges offer more points than others. Members should focus on completing the challenges that offer the most points to maximize their score.
  3. Participate actively: To make the most of Clan Games, members should actively participate throughout the event. This means checking in regularly and completing challenges whenever possible.
  4. Be considerate of others: While it's important to earn points, members should also be considerate of other members and not hog challenges. Be sure to share challenges with other members so everyone has a chance to earn points.
By participating actively and working together as a team, we can earn enough points to reach the target and receive the rewards offered in Clan Games.

9. Promotion and Demotion

Promotion and demotion in the clan are important tools for maintaining a healthy and active community. As a clan, we want to ensure that our members are active, contributing, and working towards the common goals of the clan. Here are some guidelines for promotion and demotion:

  1. Promotion: Promotion is based on a member's performance and contribution to the clan. Members who show consistent activity, participate in clan wars, donate troops, and help other members will be considered for promotion. The clan leader or co-leaders will evaluate each member's performance and determine if they are eligible for promotion.
  2. Demotion: Demotion is based on a member's inactivity or violation of clan rules. Members who are inactive for an extended period of time or who consistently break clan rules will be considered for demotion. The clan leader or co-leaders will evaluate each member's performance and determine if demotion is necessary.
  3. Communication: Communication is key when it comes to promotion and demotion. If a member has questions about their status or feels that they have been unfairly demoted or not promoted, they should communicate with the clan leader or co-leaders. We value open communication and want to ensure that everyone feels heard and respected.
  4. Fairness: We strive to be fair and objective when it comes to promotion and demotion. All members will be evaluated based on their performance and contribution to the clan, and decisions will be made based on these factors alone.
By following these guidelines, we can ensure that our clan is active, healthy, and working towards common goals. Let's all work together to contribute to the clan and support each other in our efforts.


10. Kick

Kicking a member is never a decision that should be taken lightly, but there are certain situations where it may be necessary to maintain the health and activity of the clan. Here are some reasons why a member may be kicked from the clan:

  1. Inactivity: Members who are inactive for an extended period of time may be kicked from the clan. This is because inactive members do not contribute to the clan's progress and can slow down our overall activity level.
  2. Breaking clan rules: Members who consistently break clan rules may be kicked from the clan. These rules are in place to ensure the health and success of the clan, and violations can harm the clan's progress and reputation.
  3. Leaving without Permission: If a member leaves the clan without informing our leaders, it can create confusion and disrupt our activities. As such, we have a policy that any member who leaves the clan without informing our leaders may be permanently banned from our clan.
  4. Failure to participate: Members who consistently fail to participate in clan wars, Clan Games, and other clan activities may be kicked from the clan. Active participation is critical to maintaining a healthy and successful clan.
  5. Toxic behavior: Members who engage in toxic behavior, such as bullying or discrimination, may be kicked from the clan. Toxic behavior can harm the morale of the clan and create a negative atmosphere.
It is important to note that kicking a member should be a last resort, and efforts should be made to communicate with the member and provide opportunities for improvement before resorting to a kick. Additionally, the decision to kick a member should be made by the clan leader or co-leaders, with input from other members as necessary.

By maintaining clear guidelines and communication, we can create a healthy and active clan where everyone feels welcome and supported.
